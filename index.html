Memento

A masterpiece from Christopher Nolan

The Story

Leonard (Guy Pearce) is tracking down the man who raped and murdered his wife. The difficulty, however, of locating his wife's killer is compounded by the fact that he suffers from a rare, untreatable form of memory loss. Although he can recall details of life before his accident, Leonard cannot remember what happened fifteen minutes ago, where he's going, or why.
"Memento" is rated R for occasional strong profanity, violence (a brutal beating, as well as some gunfire), graphic gore, use of crude sexual slang terms, brief simulated drug use (crack cocaine) and fleeting nudity.

The Director

Christopher Edward Nolan CBE (born 30 July 1970) is a British and American filmmaker. Known for their Hollywood blockbusters with complex storytelling, Nolan is considered a leading filmmaker of the 21st century; Their films have grossed $5 billion worldwide. The recipient of many accolades, They has been nominated for five Academy Awards, five BAFTA Awards and six Golden Globe Awards. In 2015, They was listed as one of the 100 most influential people in the world by Time, and in 2019, They was appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire for Their contributions to film.
