Virginia Woolf


Who was she?

   Virginia Woolf was an English writer, considered one of the most
   important modernist 20th-century authors and a pioneer in the use of
   stream of consciousness as a narrative device.
   She was home-schooled in English classics and Victorian literature from
   a young age. From 1897 to 1901, she attended the Ladies' Department of
   King's College London, where she studied classics and history and came
   into contact with early reformers of women's higher education and the
   women's rights movement.
